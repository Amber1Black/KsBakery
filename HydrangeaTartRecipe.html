What to do

Tart Crust :

  • Take the butter out from the fridge to let it soften
  • Add icing sugar by batches to mix well together
  • Add egg by 2 times to mix together
  • Add almond powder to mix together
  • Add corn starch with seiving it before mixing together
  • Add plain flour by batches with seiving it to mix well together
  • Roll out the dough to freeze it to make it harden to do the shaping

    [Would be better to roll out the dough with a pair of rulers, for an even thickness. Freeze for at least a few hours to let the dough get harden, in order to do the shaping more easily. It can be freezed for a week before baking.]
  • Brush the tart ring with butter, then place the dough into it.

Cake Layer :

  • Take the butter out from the fridge to let it soften
  • Add caster sugar by 2-3 times to mix well together
  • Add egg by 2 times to mix together
  • Add almond powder to mix together
  • Add plain flour by batches with seiving it to mix well together

Cream :

  • Take the cream cheese out from the fridge to let it soften
  • Add double cream, and icing sugar with seiving it to mix together

    [Pick low to medium speed of the mixer in order not to let the cream get rough]
  • Separate the cream by half, then add any color gel you like to one of the portion.

Tart :

  • Pre-heat the oven for 3 minutes at 240℃, then bake the tart crust for 12 minutes at 150℃.

    [The color would change a bit, but far from golden]
  • Fill in the cake batter to bake for another 15 minutes at 150℃

    [It will rise so just fill in half of the tart crust height]
  • When the tarts have turned to golden color, take them out of the oven to let them cool down
  • Wait for the tarts to be completely cooled down, to add cream on top, then place them into the fridge for 30 minutes at least.
  • Put some cream in the middle to form a small semi-sphere
  • Use nozzle 2D to pipe the hydrangea on top
